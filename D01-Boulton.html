William Boulton's Design Principles

Key Design Principles

  • Listen to user feedback.
  • Include accessibility options for users.
  • Have consistency across all pages and menus.
  • All content should be visible to the users.
  • Use constraints to prevent users from accessing certain options.

Conceptual Design Principles

  • Have a purpose when designing the conceptual model.
  • Let the technology of the product be determined by the model, not the model determined by the technology.
  • Avoid creating models that are too complicated.
  • Ensure that the model is in line with the original vision of the product.
  • If a model no longer fits the vision of the product, rework it.

User Focused Design Principles

  • Do not make too many system changes all at once.
  • Be mindful of how changes will affect the current user experience.
  • If a new version must be released, allow users to stay on the old version for a certain amount of time.
  • Put the user first.
  • Design with the user's needs in mind.
  • Allow your product to communicate with its users.
  • Anticipate the expectations of your users.

Accesibility Design Principles

  • Be mindful of the different ways users will experience your product.
  • Include accessible alternatives for certain actions.
  • Avoid the use of flashing lights and loud noises.
  • Make your product as understandable and robust as possible.
  • Ensure that your product is always operable.

Technical Design Principles

  • Ensure the code of the program is well formatted and well commented.
  • Use automatic testing to speed up the testing process.
  • Reduce the chance of user error by as much as possible.
  • If an error does occur, take the blame away from the user.
  • Create error messages that are not vague and explain the problem.
  • Allow users to report any problems they encounter.

Cosmetic Design Principles

  • Use a logo that is easy to read and works well when it is large or small.
  • Use a distinct color scheme throughout your entire product.
  • When choosing a font, choose one that is readable but captures the vision of your product.
  • Avoid using colors that don't work well together, such as yellow and white.
  • Don't cram too much information in one spot - space it out.
  • Ensure that the product looks consistent across different devices.
  • Have icons that are readable and easy to understand at a glance.
